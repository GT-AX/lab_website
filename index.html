Research Areas

Autonomous Assembly

Developing robust control algorithms for flexible robotic assembly lines operating alongside human workers safely and efficiently. Our research focuses on advancing multi-agent coordination, real-time computer vision for component tracking, and safety-critical reinforcement learning. By combining these, we aim to transform traditional rigid manufacturing into dynamic, human-robot collaborative environments where systems can adapt to disruptions on-the-fly without compromising factory throughput or worker safety.

Manufacturing ML

Applying deep learning and predictive analytics to optimize supply chains, predict failures, and improve product quality consistency. We utilize large-scale industrial IoT data to train models that can detect anomalies in milliseconds, preventing costly downtime before it cascade across the line. Moreover, our focus extends to federated learning paradigms to allow multiple geographically distributed automated facilities to share predictive intelligence without compromising proprietary process data.

Sustainable Materials

Innovating low-waste forming processes and evaluating new high-performance composite materials for aerospace and automotive sectors. Our lab experiments with bio-based resins, life-cycle tracking tags embedded in materials, and advanced carbon-fiber recycling techniques to effectively close the loop on modern manufacturing lifecycles. We conduct both computational material science simulations and extensive physical stress testing to validate these eco-friendly alternatives directly against conventional industry standards.
